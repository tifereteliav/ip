Below is a practical, clinic-ready framework for designing a questionnaire / digital decision-support app that helps adults with type 1 diabetes choose between the Medtronic 780G, Tandem t:slim X2/Mobi, and Omnipod 5. DESIGN PRINCIPLES Your questionnaire/app should: ⭐ Be short (5–7 minutes) Patients lose engagement in long surveys. Use branching logic so they only see relevant questions. ⭐ Use plain language Avoid algorithm jargon. Provide tooltips or short explanations. ⭐ Match patient needs → device features Each answer should directly map to characteristics of pumps (e.g., tubeless preference → favors Omnipod 5). ⭐ End with a personalized recommendation Either: Top 1–2 pump options with rationale, or A “pump compatibility profile” summarizing fit across domains. 2. CORE DOMAINS TO INCLUDE From clinical practice and the Panther comparison tool, these are the domains that matter most: 1️⃣ Lifestyle & Wearability Preferences Tubed vs tubeless Comfort wearing a device on body Ability/willingness to carry a phone Sports/exercise style Travel, swimming, work environment 2️⃣ Tech Preferences & Phone Ecosystem iPhone vs Android Desire to bolus from phone Comfort with app-based operation Need for remote monitoring (caregivers, partners) 3️⃣ Glucose Management Style Importance of automated boluses Preference for adjustable targets Desire to tune settings (I:C, CF, basal profiles) Use of sleep/activity modes 4️⃣ Current CGM Preference Dexcom (G6/G7) Guardian 4 Glooko/Discover/Tidepool/Beta Bionics data ecosystem 5️⃣ Hypoglycemia/hyperglycemia concerns Fear of lows Need for aggressive correction algorithms Need for strong automation during meals/overnight 6️⃣ Insurance / cost / availability (Optional in app; essential in clinic workflow.) 3.
